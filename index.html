Doctoral & Master's Work

Research

Doctoral Research · PAMP-Induced Tau Pathology & Neuroinflammation

PS19 tauopathy mice received four PAMPs targeting TLR2/3/4/9 pathways monthly over four months. Tissue was collected 30 days after the final challenge to assess long-lasting rather than acute inflammatory effects. Primary researcher across CMU and MSU-GRRC in collaboration with Dr. David Morgan. First-author manuscript currently in final preparation for submission.

Primary Finding

  • Repeated peripheral immune challenges drove a 5.4-fold hippocampal p-tau (AT8) elevation in PS19 mice at 30 days post-challenge, with total tau (HT7) remaining unaltered, confirming post-translational hyperphosphorylation rather than increased transgene expression

Supporting Finding

  • Significant genotype × treatment interaction for MHC II and strong positive Spearman correlations between hippocampal p-tau and glial reactivity markers (MHC II: r=0.523, GFAP: r=0.556, CD68: r=0.501), suggesting tau pathology and peripheral immune activation converge to amplify neuroinflammatory responses

Master's Thesis · Prenatal Inflammatory Stress & Amyloid Burden

5xFAD transgenic mice born from transgenic or wild-type mothers were used to examine the effect of prenatal inflammatory exposure on hippocampal amyloid plaque burden and microglial density. Effect of maternal genotype analyzed within each sex. Master's thesis research, CMU.

Primary Finding

  • Prenatal inflammatory stress paradoxically reduced hippocampal amyloid burden by ~52% in 5xFAD offspring of transgenic mothers, with the effect reaching significance independently in both male (46%, P=.026) and female (59%, P=.015) offspring

Supporting Finding

  • Microglial density was not significantly altered by maternal genotype in either brain region or sex, suggesting the plaque reduction reflects altered amyloid processing rather than increased microglial clearance; females exhibited greater overall hippocampal amyloid burden than males independent of maternal genotype (F=8.4, P=.011)

Technical Skills

Animal Models

Transgenic and knock-in AD models (3xTg, 5xFAD, PS19, APP-KI NL-F/NL-G-F, MAPT-KI); Parkinson's (LRRK1/LRRK2-KO); inducible cell-cycle re-entry (TAg/OFF NCCR); AAV-mediated human tau seeding (bilateral hippocampus and anterior cortex).

Surgical & In Vivo Procedures

Stereotaxic surgery (cannula, osmotic pump, AAV injection); transcardial perfusion (4% PFA); brain microdissection; IP injection; blood collection and serum fractionation; pharmacological microglial depletion (PLX5622/CSF1R); anesthesia (isoflurane, pentobarbital); behavioral testing: open field, MWM, EPM, NOR, SOR, Y-maze, hindlimb clasp (EthoVision XT, ANY-maze, HK MotorMonitor).

Histology & Tissue Processing

Tissue sectioning (vibratome, microtome, cryostat); antigen retrieval (heat-induced/citrate, formic acid, enzymatic/trypsin); immunohistochemistry (DAB, IF, multi-label co-staining); Nissl staining; lipophilic neuronal tracing (DiI); cryopreservation.

Microscopy, Stereology & Image Analysis

Confocal microscopy (Nikon A1R); widefield microscopy (ZEISS Axio Scan.Z1, ZEISS Axio Imager M1); unbiased stereology (MBF Stereo Investigator: Optical Fractionator, Area Fraction Fractionator, Cavalieri); dendritic morphology analysis (MBF Neurolucida); image analysis (ImageJ, FIJI, NearCYTE).

Molecular Biology & Cell Culture

PCR (nested, multiplex; primer design); qRT-PCR (multiplex TaqMan); Western blot; ELISA (sandwich); primary cell culture (NSCs, MSCs, microglia, neurons); FACS; ICC; dose-response, proliferation, migration, and wound healing assays; C. elegans (introductory).

Biostatistics & Computation

R programming; GraphPad Prism; SPSS; GitHub Pages. Non-parametric methods (Mann-Whitney U, Brunner-Munzel, Aligned Rank Transform ANOVA, permutation tests, Spearman correlation); normality testing (Shapiro-Wilk, Anderson-Darling); effect size reporting (Cliff's Delta, Hodges-Lehmann, Probability Index, Partial Eta/Omega Squared); bootstrapped confidence intervals (5,000–10,000 resamples); Holm-corrected post-hoc contrasts; data visualization.

Research

PAMP-Induced Tau Pathology & Neuroinflammation

PS19 tauopathy mice received four PAMPs targeting TLR2/3/4/9 pathways monthly over four months. Tissue was collected 30 days after the final challenge to assess long-lasting rather than acute inflammatory effects. Primary researcher across CMU and MSU-GRRC in collaboration with Dr. David Morgan. First-author manuscript currently in final preparation for submission.

Key Findings

  • 5.4-fold hippocampal p-tau (AT8) elevation vs. vehicle controls at 30 days post-challenge, confirming post-translational hyperphosphorylation rather than increased transgene expression (HT7 unaltered)
  • Significant genotype × treatment interaction for MHC II in hippocampus and whole-hemisphere, suggesting tau pathology and peripheral immune activation act synergistically to amplify microglial antigen presentation
  • Strong positive Spearman correlations between hippocampal p-tau and glial reactivity markers (MHC II: r=0.523, GFAP: r=0.556, CD68: r=0.501), supporting a self-reinforcing relationship between tau pathology and neuroinflammatory activation

Prenatal Inflammatory Stress & Amyloid Burden

5xFAD transgenic mice born from transgenic or wild-type mothers were used to examine the effect of prenatal inflammatory exposure on hippocampal amyloid plaque burden and microglial density. Effect of maternal genotype analyzed within each sex. Master's thesis research, CMU.

Key Findings

  • Prenatal inflammatory stress reduced hippocampal amyloid burden by 46% in males and 59% in females, with the effect reaching significance in both sexes (males: P=.026, females: P=.015)
  • Significant sex difference in plaque area: females exhibited greater overall amyloid burden than males independent of maternal genotype (F=8.4, P=.011)
  • Microglial density was not significantly altered by maternal genotype in either brain region or sex, suggesting reduced plaque burden may reflect altered amyloid processing rather than increased microglial clearance
